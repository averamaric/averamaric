03 — Supply Chain Challenge

Multiple Suppliers,
One Drawing

In traditional manufacturing, companies often subcontract aspects of production to multiple suppliers of the same component to reduce supply chain disruption risk. However, this introduces a critical challenge: variability and interchangeability.

Will all components from different suppliers be consistent and interchangeable? Will they fit together during assembly, or must they be matched and batched per supplier?

This project directly addressed this challenge through the "master parts" provided with the CP Lab line — reference casings from an existing supplier against which our parts were measured and validated.

🔍 Key Findings

  • Even with identical CAD files, print parameters across two different printers (Ultimaker vs Elegoo) produced measurable dimensional variation
  • The same printer with different filament batches showed slight colour and shrinkage differences
  • Configuration 7 (15% infill, no supports) produced the best fit with master parts, despite not having the highest infill
  • Statistical Process Control (SPC) would be required to monitor and maintain consistency across multiple suppliers

📌 Recommendation: Implement regular gauge R&R studies and maintain digital twins of each supplier's process parameters to ensure interchangeability.

05 — Key Takeaways

Lessons
Learned

'01 Digital Manufacturing 3D printing enables rapid iteration — design changes tested in hours. However, for 50,000 units/year, FDM requires ~93 printers. Recommendation: injection moulding for high volume, additive for prototyping.
'02 Measurement Challenges Thickness tolerance (1.6-2.2mm) was achieved (1.7mm), but variability between manual calipers and CP Lab inspection station was observed. Automated in-line measurement is essential.
'03 Multi-Supplier Variability Even with identical drawings, different printers/settings produce variation. Master parts are essential reference standards. Digital traceability (QR codes) enables root cause analysis.
'04 Plant Simulation Benefits Virtual modelling identified the bottleneck: 3D printing throughput (228 printers needed), not assembly line. Simulation prevented costly physical trial-and-error.

🎯 Final recommendation: For 50,000 units/year, consider batch printing (4+ pairs per plate) or transition to injection moulding for the casings while keeping additive for custom variants.
