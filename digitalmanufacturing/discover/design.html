Diseño 3D

Complete design process in Fusion 360, from engineering drawing to print-ready files. This entry documents every design decision, dimensional iteration, and the critical link between CAD parameters and assembly line performance.

01 — Concepto inicial

De la Idea
al Modelo

The project began with full engineering drawings for both top and bottom phone casings. No tolerances were specified initially, only the requirement that the final parts must successfully navigate the complete CP Lab assembly line, including dimensional verification stations, component insertion tests, and seamless fit with the pre-existing "master parts" provided with the production line.

Both casings were modeled from scratch in Fusion 360 using direct modeling. When drawing information was incomplete (e.g., certain internal radii), design decisions followed the pattern of similar geometric features elsewhere on the casing to maintain visual and functional consistency.

A key early insight: print parameters would ultimately determine whether the parts passed the thickness tolerance (1.6-2.2mm differential between two measurement points on the bottom case) and component fit requirements.
