VerdictUnnecessarily expensive
Why P1S/P2S? The 256×256mm build plate allows arranging 3 columns × 4 rows of 11×6cm parts (with ~1cm margin between parts) = 12 parts per cycle. Its enclosure ensures stable temperature (less warping), AI detects failures and allows continuing to print unaffected parts, and its price makes it viable in a fleet. The P2S adds a touchscreen and better extrusion system, but the P1S (now on sale ~£399) is equally valid for production.

Based on the simulator scenario (selected shift and failure rate). CAPEX = hardware. OPEX = annual operating cost.

Cost notes:
· PLA in UK: £15–25/kg for medium-high quality filament. Volume purchasing can reduce this cost by 20–30%.
· UK electricity: ~£0.24–0.34/kWh (industrial rate). P1S consumes ~100–130W during active printing.
· Hardware CAPEX does not include shelving, wiring, ventilation system, or maintenance tools.
· Annual maintenance cost (~5–8% of hardware) is not included in this calculation.
