Why P1S/P2S? The 256×256mm build plate can fit 2 columns × 3 rows of 115×65mm parts (including margin) = 6 parts (3 pairs) per cycle. Enclosure ensures temperature stability (less warping), AI detects failures and allows continuing unaffected parts, and price makes it viable for a fleet. P2S adds a touch screen and better extrusion system, but P1S (~£399 on sale) is equally valid for production.
Step 2 — Process parameters
Process
Data

Actual confirmed data:
· 1 pair → 3h 42m · 52.1g PLA
· 2 pairs → 7h 24m (linear ×2) · 104.2g
· 3 pairs → ~11h 06m (linear ×3) · 156.3g

Build plate 256×256mm · part 110×60mm + 5mm margin
Max layout: 2 cols × 3 rows = 6 parts = 3 pairs max.
Why linear scaling? Top and bottom print simultaneously on the same plate. Adding more pairs increases the geometry per layer, scaling time proportionally. Confirmed: 1 pair = 3h42m, 2 pairs = 7h24m (exactly ×2). For 3 pairs, estimated ×3 = ~11h06m — validate in slicer before using this scenario.

Based on the simulator scenario (shift and failure selected). CAPEX = hardware. OPEX = annual operating cost.

Cost notes:
· PLA in UK: £15–25/kg for mid-to-high quality filament. Bulk purchasing can reduce this cost by 20–30%.
· UK electricity: ~£0.24–0.34/kWh (industrial rate). P1S consumes ~100–130W during active printing.
· Hardware CAPEX does not include shelving, wiring, ventilation, or maintenance tools.
· Annual maintenance cost (~5–8% of hardware) is not included in this calculation.
