The Story of
Madres & Artesanas

A journey of heritage, hands, and heart. From the highlands of Bolivia to the world's most distinguished runways.

Where It All Began

In the vibrant, bustling markets of La Paz, a silent revolution was being woven. Madres & Artesanas Tex was born not from a business plan, but from a simple observation: extraordinary talent, passed down through generations of women, was hidden behind the need for economic opportunity.

We started in 2015 with just five artisans, a single shared loom, and a belief that the world deserved to see the beauty they could create. Our first collection was small, but it carried the weight of centuries of tradition.

The Hands Behind the Thread

Each stitch tells a story. Our master artisans, like Juana and her three daughters, don't just knit; they interpret. They adapt ancient patterns of the Aymara and Quechua cultures into contemporary designs, ensuring that every garment is a living piece of cultural heritage.

We are not a factory; we are a collective. A network of over 100 women who find in this work not just a salary, but a platform for their artistry and a community that supports them.

“We are not preserving the past. We are weaving the future, one thread at a time.”

— Ana Maria, Founder

A Philosophy of slowness

In a world obsessed with speed, we choose slowness. A single M&A Tex sweater can take up to a week to complete. This is not inefficiency; it is dedication. It means our artisans work from their homes, at their own pace, without the pressure of an assembly line.

This philosophy extends to our environment. We use only natural, locally-sourced fibers and low-impact dyes, ensuring that our craft honors the land that inspires it. Slow fashion is not a trend for us; it is the only way.

From Our Loom to Your Collection

Today, our yarns have traveled far beyond the Andes. We partner with discerning designers and brands who share our values: a respect for craft, a desire for authenticity, and a commitment to ethical production. Our atelier works in co-creation, translating your vision into tangible, luxurious textures.

Whether it's a delicate beaded appliqué for a New York fashion week piece or a chunky hand-knit for a European capsule collection, our work remains rooted in the same principle: to empower the women who make it possible.
